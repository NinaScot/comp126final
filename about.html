A bit about me

As a kid, I wanted nothing more than to be a paleontologist. I read every book in the school library on dinosaurs, watched as many dinosaur movies as I could find, and spent many long hours tearing up the yard in search of the many fossils I was sure were waiting just beneath the surface. My parents however, were quick to save the lawn by telling me all the fossils had already been found when the house was being built and by relegating my searches to the decorative rock pile by the garage. But it would take a lot more than that to convince me.

Even though I wasn’t allowed to set up dig sites anymore, I kept up my quest, scouring the rocks for treasures and marking them up with chalk to look like dinosaur bones when I wanted to give up. But one day I was rewarded. Among the hundreds of tiny stones lay the holy grail, the imprint of a fern on a stone chip. My parents didn’t believe me until they saw it themselves, but there it was, clear as day upon the rich warmth of the ancient clay.

That tiny rock made a huge impact. It taught me to persevere, even if nobody else thinks you can do it. This has been my driving force in life and ultimately what has led me to create and run my own shop. Your only limits are the ones you set, so walk in the sun, hold your head high, and make a little history along the way!
